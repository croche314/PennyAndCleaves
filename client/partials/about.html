About Us

In 1892, John Penny began a bespoke tailor shop with his then apprentice Nathaniel Cleaves in London's famous Saville Row district. After months of lackluster sales, Penny and Cleaves were on the verge of bankruptcy when one of their unassuming hats became a hot, must-have item among local businessmen. Nicknamed the 'Dorchester', the success of this simple yet dapper hat convinced the two business partners that their future lie not in suit making but men's fashion in general.

Post World War 1, Penny and Cleaves had expanded into almost a dozen stores around greater London. With the American economy booming, they decided to set up shop "across the pond" and opened their first American store in New York City in 1922. Shortly thereafter, the first Penny and Cleaves catalog was distributed and American catalog orders soon became a primary source of revenue for the company.

John Penny died in 1939 following a stroke. His son, Bertram, took his place as CEO and aggressively expanded both the retail and catalog footprint of P&C and by 1955 the company was grossing sales of more than $4 million annually. Nathaniel Cleaves died in 1949 from pneumonia. His share of the business was bequeathed to his eldest son who had no interest in following in his father's footsteps as a clothier but remained on the board of directors until his death in 1980.

In 1971, CEO Sarah Jensen (descendant of John and Bertram Penny) took the company away from a direct-to-consumer model instead opting to license Penny & Cleaves clothing to major department stores. This allowed P&C to focus less on day-to-day retail and catalog operations and more on what's always been at the heart of Penny & Cleaves: quality men's fashion.

Today, Penny and Cleaves combines the quality and craftsmanship of its founders with a modern, youthful style. Since its inception in 1892, P&C has had at least one Penny and one Cleaves on its payroll, a tradition integral to marrying the company's past with its present and future.
